# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of this project, commit to making participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that build an open, welcoming, diverse, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

The following behaviors contribute to a positive environment:

- Using language that is welcoming and inclusive toward all participants
- Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and lived experiences
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback in good faith
- Accepting responsibility when we make mistakes, offering apologies to those affected, and learning from the experience
- Prioritizing the long-term health of the community over individual preferences

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

- Sexualized language or imagery of any kind, or unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, deliberately provocative comments, or personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing another person's private information—physical address, email address, or similar—without their explicit consent
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider unprofessional or harmful in a collaborative technical environment

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They have the authority and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Maintainers will communicate their moderation decisions where appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussion boards, and any official communication channels. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces—for example, using an official email address, posting from an official social media account, or acting as a designated representative at an event.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers at **templetwo@proton.me**. All reports will be reviewed promptly and investigated with discretion. The maintainers are obligated to protect the privacy and safety of the reporter. Further details about specific enforcement policies may be made available separately.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent consequences as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will use the following four-tier ladder to determine consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of language or behavior that is inappropriate for a professional environment or is mildly unwelcoming.

**Consequence:** A private, written message from a maintainer explaining the nature of the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A single incident or pattern of behavior that crosses professional boundaries or causes disruption to other community members.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with specific conditions about continued participation. No unsolicited interaction with the people involved—including those enforcing the Code of Conduct—for a specified period. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior or targeted harassment of an individual.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from all forms of interaction or public communication within the project for a specified period. No public or private contact with the people involved is permitted during this time. Violating these terms may result in a permanent ban.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** A pattern of sustained violations of community standards, including repeated harassment, aggression toward individuals or groups, or documented bad-faith behavior across multiple incidents.

**Consequence:** Permanent exclusion from all forms of public participation in the project.
